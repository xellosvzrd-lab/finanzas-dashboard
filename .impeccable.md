## Design Context

### Users
Daniel and Ama — a couple living in Argentina who built this tool for themselves. They use it both ways: quick check-ins from the phone (morning commute, after a purchase) and deliberate reviews at the laptop (weekend budgeting, end of month). The app is private and personal — no external audience, no onboarding. Every session is by someone who already knows it well.

### Brand Personality
**Warm. Intimate. Modern.**

This is a tool made with love, used with trust. It should feel like something you're proud to have built — not like a generic SaaS dashboard. The financial data lives here, but the experience should feel close and personal, like a well-designed journal you actually reach for.

Emotional goal: *opening this app should feel good, not obligatory.*

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme:** Light. Daytime use, personal context, couple's tool. The warm beige palette already makes the right call — lean into it.
- **Palette:** The terracotta accent (#C8845A) is exactly right. Keep it as the single warm note. Neutrals should be tinted faintly toward it (OKLCH, chroma ~0.008). No AI palette: no cyan, no purple gradients.
- **Typography:** Two-font system. **Bricolage Grotesque** (Google Fonts) for headings and display — it has personality and warmth without feeling decorative. **Libre Franklin** (Google Fonts) for body/UI text — humanist, legible, approachable. Do NOT use Inter anywhere. The contrast between these two creates the "editorial personal" quality the app should have.
- **Motion:** Subtle and purposeful. Entry animations under 300ms. The staggered KPI card reveals are good — keep and refine. No bounce, no elastic. Ease-out-quart.
- **Layout:** Asymmetric where it creates hierarchy. Breathe generously in key areas (Mi mes page especially). Not everything needs a card.

### Design Principles
1. **Personal over generic.** Built by two people, for two people. Every detail should feel intentional and hand-crafted, not templated.
2. **Warmth in the details.** The terracotta palette is the personality anchor. Typography, spacing, and interactions should reinforce that warmth — not fight it.
3. **Calm confidence.** Financial data can create anxiety. The UI should feel settled, clear, and reassuring. Information first, decoration second.
4. **Effortless at a glance.** Used daily — the most important question ("how am I doing this month?") should be answered before you consciously look for it.
5. **Dual-context fluency.** Just as satisfying on a phone during lunch as on a laptop Sunday morning. Mobile is not a degraded experience.
